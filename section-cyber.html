🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Notable

"SleeperGem" supply-chain attack hijacks dormant RubyGems maintainer accounts

📅 Jul 19, 2026

Aikido Security researcher Charlie Eriksen found a new gem, git_credential_manager, built over nine hours across four rapid releases into a working dropper that fetches a payload from a lookalike Forgejo host and quietly skips CI environments to target developer laptops instead. The same publisher pushed malicious updates to two other long-dormant gems, including fastlane-plugin-run_tests_firebase_testlab with 574,661 downloads under a different maintainer entirely.

Why it matters: RubyGems had mostly avoided the account-takeover wave that's hit npm and PyPI for over a year — a reactivated account with zero recent activity is exactly the kind of "boring" account defenders should flag, not ignore, across every package registry your build pulls from.

Critical

Volexity: SonicWall SMA1000 zero-days were exploited for root access weeks before disclosure

📅 Jul 17, 2026

Volexity is tracking a previously undocumented threat actor, UTA0533, that chained CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0 SSRF) and CVE-2026-15410 to plant a setuid root-run binary on SMA1000 appliances as early as June 22 — nearly a month before SonicWall's public advisory. The group then injected a custom webshell (ORANGETAIL) and the open-source Suo5 proxy tool into a legitimate SonicWall process to tunnel traffic and harvest credentials.

Why it matters: "Patch by the federal deadline" doesn't retroactively evict an attacker who's had root since before the CVE existed — if you run SMA1000, treat this as a confirmed-compromise investigation, not a patch-and-move-on item, and hunt for Volexity's published IOCs.

Ernst & Young discloses tax-client data breach via third-party support system

📅 Jul 17, 2026

EY says an unauthorized party accessed a third-party IT support-ticket platform between March 28 and April 12, downloading documents that included personal and financial data used in client tax filings; the intrusion wasn't flagged internally until April 23. The Big Four firm hasn't disclosed the affected system, exact record count, or whether the breach extends beyond U.S. clients, and is offering 24 months of Experian identity monitoring.

Why it matters: A three-week dwell time inside a support-ticket tool that routinely handled attached tax documents is a reminder that "low-risk" internal IT platforms often carry the same sensitive data as your crown-jewel systems — vendor risk reviews should ask what gets attached to tickets, not just what the tool is rated for.

Lidl notifies online-shop customers across three countries of a third-party breach

📅 Jul 10, 2026

Lidl began emailing Online Shop customers in Germany, Belgium and the Netherlands after an incident at an unnamed IT service provider exposed names, phone numbers, email addresses, dates of birth and customer numbers; the retailer says passwords, payment details and delivery addresses were not affected and has notified the Dutch Data Protection Authority.

Why it matters: The exposed fields — name, DOB, phone, email — are exactly what's needed to craft a convincing retailer-impersonation phishing lure; expect targeted SMS/email fraud attempts against the affected customer base in the weeks ahead.

🛡️ Vulnerabilities & Patches
Critical

15-year-old nginx bug enables pre-auth remote code execution (CVE-2026-42533)

📅 Jul 19, 2026

The flaw has been silently exploitable since March 2011, when nginx's map directive gained regex support: a shared, mutable capture-state array can be overwritten mid-request, causing a heap buffer overflow and an ASLR-defeating pointer leak. F5 shipped fixes in nginx 1.30.4/1.31.3 and NGINX Plus R36 P7/37.0.3.1; researchers achieved 10-for-10 exploitation on Ubuntu 24.04 with ASLR enabled and are withholding full PoC details for 21 days post-patch.

Why it matters: A CVE this old, this widely deployed, and this reliably exploitable is a "patch this week, not this quarter" situation — nginx sits in front of an enormous share of the internet's web traffic, and the 21-day disclosure grace period is a rapidly closing window, not a reason to wait.

Critical

CISA adds actively-exploited AD FS and SharePoint zero-days from record Patch Tuesday to KEV

📅 Jul 17, 2026

Two of the zero-days Microsoft patched in July's 622-flaw Patch Tuesday were already under active attack: CVE-2026-56164, an unauthenticated SharePoint Server privilege-escalation bug, and CVE-2026-56155, an Active Directory Federation Services elevation-of-privilege flaw. CISA's KEV listing gives federal agencies until July 28 to remediate the AD FS bug, on top of the July 19 deadline already set for the SharePoint deserialization flaw added the prior week.

Why it matters: Two actively-exploited zero-days landing in the same Patch Tuesday, on top of an already-exploited SharePoint chain from weeks earlier, means SharePoint and AD FS environments need a full audit of exposed on-prem instances — not just the newest patch — before assuming you're covered.

Oracle E-Business Suite flaw added to KEV after zero-day exploitation traced to June 27

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Defused's honeypot decoys recorded the first in-the-wild exploitation of CVE-2026-46817 (CVSS 9.8) on June 27 — six weeks after Oracle's patch and before any public proof-of-concept existed — via the Oracle Payments ibytransmit endpoint, which lets unauthenticated attackers read arbitrary server files. Shadowserver now counts over 450 exposed EBS instances online, and CISA has since added the CVE to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog.

Why it matters: Six weeks of silent zero-day exploitation before any public PoC existed is a reminder that "no exploit code available" is not the same as "not being exploited" — honeypot telemetry increasingly beats public disclosure as the earliest signal, and it's worth watching sources that publish it directly.

🤖 AI Security
Critical

Trend Micro: hacker used Gemini CLI as the primary operator of a live botnet

📅 Jul 16, 2026

TrendAI Research analyzed 200+ Gemini CLI session logs from a Russian-speaking actor who spoke instructions in conversational Russian while the AI wrote server code, deployed it to a fresh VPS, configured Cloudflare tunnels and brought a command-and-control server for eight compromised dental-clinic PCs back online — start to finish in six minutes, with the human contributing just 11% of the actual work.

Why it matters: When infrastructure rebuild time drops from hours to minutes and the AI can regenerate fresh malware/C2 logic on demand, static IOCs and infrastructure blocklists lose most of their value fast — detection needs to shift toward behavior and identity, not indicators that expire in six minutes.

Notable

"MemGhost" research: a single email can plant persistent false memories in AI agents

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Researchers behind the "When Claws Remember but Do Not Tell" paper built a reinforcement-learning framework that converts one poisoned email into a planted, hidden "fact" an agent later treats as true — succeeding in 87.5% of background-mode runs against OpenClaw on GPT-5.4 and 71.4% against a Claude Code SDK agent on Sonnet 4.6, across 56 fresh test cases.

Why it matters: Memory-enabled agents that persist "facts" across sessions create a new class of long-lived poisoning that a one-time prompt-injection fix won't catch — any agent with write access to its own memory store needs that store audited like a config file, not trusted like a cache.

Notable

"PromptFiction" flaw let a single click auto-submit hidden prompts in Claude Desktop

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Oasis Security found that Claude Desktop's claude:// URL handler skipped the review step the web app enforces, so a crafted link could open the app and submit an attacker's full prompt automatically — concealed below a "show more" fold — with no confirmation screen. Combined with filesystem-connected MCP servers, the flaw could plant persistence in files a user later asked Claude to edit; Anthropic fixed it in Claude Desktop 1.1.2321.

Why it matters: This is Oasis's second Claude Desktop disclosure this year (after "Claudy Day" in March) following the same pattern — a convenience feature that skips a confirmation step becomes an attacker's automatic-execution primitive; audit any custom URI-scheme handler your AI desktop tools register.

Cloudflare launches "Precursor," continuous behavioral bot detection across entire sessions

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Precursor replaces one-time CAPTCHA-style checks with ongoing analysis of mouse movement, keystroke dynamics and navigation behavior throughout a browsing session, aiming to catch automation that passes an initial challenge but behaves non-human later on. It's available as a one-click deployment across Cloudflare's existing bot-management customer base.

Why it matters: AI-driven scraping and account-abuse bots are increasingly good at clearing static checks, so the shift toward continuous, session-long behavioral scoring is a trend worth matching against your own bot-management stack rather than a one-off vendor launch.
