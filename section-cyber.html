🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Major

EU and UK launch first joint cyber sanctions package, naming Russia's FSB Centre 16 and Turla

📅 Jul 13, 2026

The Council of the EU sanctioned nine individuals and four entities while the UK separately designated 24, including senior GRU officers, Trickbot/Conti leader Vitaly Kovalev, Lumma Stealer developers, and members tied to a failed Turla-linked strike on Poland's energy grid that could have cut power to roughly 500,000 people. It is the first time the two blocs have coordinated a cyber-sanctions package together.

Why it matters: Publicly naming a specific FSB unit as the controller behind a decade of European intrusions is a rare attribution move — it signals governments now have enough confidence to put names, not just malware families, on the record.

US Treasury sanctions First VPN Service and a "cryptor" seller for enabling ransomware attacks

📅 Jul 14, 2026

OFAC designated First VPN Service (1VPNS) and its administrator Dmytro Rashevskyi, along with Belarusian national Yegeniy Silayev, who sells malware-obfuscating "cryptor" tools. The action follows a May takedown of 1VPNS's infrastructure by European police and the FBI; officials say ransomware operations using these services caused billions in losses to US businesses, hospitals and municipal governments.

Why it matters: Sanctioning the infrastructure providers and tool sellers behind ransomware — not just the operators — targets the supply chain that lets new ransomware brands spin up quickly after takedowns.

Notable

Lidl discloses online-shop breach after an IT service provider is hacked

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Lidl is notifying online-shop customers in Germany, Belgium and the Netherlands that attackers briefly accessed a file at a third-party IT provider containing names, phone numbers, email addresses, dates of birth and customer numbers. Lidl says passwords and payment data were not in the exposed file but has not yet fully ruled out further exposure, and is warning customers to watch for phishing.

Why it matters: Another reminder that a retailer's own security posture is only as strong as its weakest outsourced IT vendor — third-party breach notifications like this one rarely name the vendor, complicating downstream risk assessment.

Nihon Kotsu, Japan's largest taxi operator, shuts down systems after malware infection

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Nihon Kotsu confirmed unauthorized external access to its internal systems over the weekend and disconnected infrastructure to contain the damage, taking down car-hire booking, reservation management and telephone dispatch — including a "labor taxi" service used by pregnant women in Tokyo and several other cities. No ransomware group has claimed responsibility, and no confirmed data leak has surfaced yet.

Why it matters: An 18,000-employee transportation operator going dark on booking and dispatch shows how quickly a single malware infection can cascade into real-world service disruption for vulnerable riders, not just an IT inconvenience.

Notable

"CrashStealer" macOS malware poses as Apple's own crash-reporting tool

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Jamf Threat Labs tracked CrashStealer from a May sample through early-July in-the-wild detections: a C++ infostealer delivered via a notarized, Gatekeeper-passing dropper that harvests browser profiles, roughly 80 cryptocurrency wallet extensions and 14 password managers, then encrypts the stolen files with AES-GCM before exfiltration to slow analysis.

Why it matters: A notarized dropper that clears Apple's own trust checks undercuts the assumption that "Apple-signed" means "safe," and the client-side encryption of stolen data is a maturing evasion trend worth tracking across other stealer families.

🛡️ Vulnerabilities & Patches
Critical

Hackers backdoor Jscrambler's npm package with a Rust infostealer targeting AI coding tools

📅 Jul 13, 2026

A threat actor used compromised publishing credentials to push malicious jscrambler npm releases (8.14–8.20) downloaded nearly 1,500 times before Jscrambler deprecated them two hours later. The payload, obfuscated with ChaCha20-Poly1305 encryption per Socket's analysis, hunted for cloud credentials, CI/CD tokens, crypto wallets, and configs for AI coding tools including Claude, Cursor and Windsurf.

Why it matters: The two-hour window shows how fast a compromised publishing credential can weaponize a trusted package — and the deliberate targeting of AI coding-tool configs marks a new, high-value theft target developers may not think to rotate.

CISA adds an 18-year-old Cisco IOS CSRF flaw to the KEV catalog

📅 Jul 13, 2026

CVE-2008-4128, a cross-site request forgery vulnerability in the HTTP administration interface of Cisco IOS 12.4 on Cisco 871 routers, joined CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a July 16 federal remediation deadline — confirmation that a bug dating to 2008 is being actively exploited nearly two decades later.

Why it matters: Ancient, unpatched network gear left running for years is exactly the kind of forgotten attack surface nation-state and criminal actors keep finding profitable — this is a good prompt to audit any legacy Cisco IOS devices still online.

📅 Jul 14, 2026

SAP released 20 security notes this Patch Day, led by CVE-2026-44747 (CVSS 9.9), a memory-corruption bug in NetWeaver Application Server ABAP that lets attackers access and modify data or crash the system. 🤖 AI Security
Critical

ClaudeBleed-linked flaw in Claude for Chrome still unpatched across eight releases, Manifold says

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Manifold reported two flaws to Anthropic on May 21, related to the mitigation shipped after the original ClaudeBleed disclosure: a malicious extension can fake the "real user click" Anthropic's fix requires to trigger Claude actions, and in the extension's autonomous "Act without asking" mode this proceeds with no visible warning, exposing Gmail, Docs and Calendar. None of the eight Claude for Chrome versions released since — including the latest 1.0.80 — appear to fix it.

Why it matters: A mitigation that survives eight patch cycles without closing the actual gap is a case study in how hard it is to retrofit trust boundaries onto an agentic browser extension after the fact — treat "fixed" claims for agent products skeptically until independently verified.

Notable

"Ghostcommit" hides prompt injection inside PNG images to steal repo secrets from AI coding agents

📅 Jul 11, 2026

Researchers at UMKC's ASSET group built a pull request that points an AGENTS.md convention file at an innocuous-looking PNG; the exploit text lives inside the image, which text-based reviewers like CodeRabbit and Bugbot never open. A coding agent later reads the picture, encodes a repo's .env secrets as integers, and commits them as a harmless-looking constant. Cursor and Antigravity leaked secrets across multiple models; Claude Code refused every time.

Why it matters: The coding harness, not the underlying model, determined whether the attack succeeded — a strong argument for evaluating the security posture of the AI coding tool itself, not just which model it's running.

Microsoft expects heavier Windows patch loads as its AI vulnerability-scanning harness finds more bugs

📅 Jul 10, 2026

Microsoft says its multi-model agentic scanning harness (MDASH) is finding more vulnerabilities in Windows source code before they become zero-days, meaning future Patch Tuesdays will bundle more fixes per release. Human engineers still review and validate every AI-suggested fix before it ships.

Why it matters: As AI-assisted bug-hunting scales at the vendor whose OS runs most enterprise endpoints, admins should expect — and plan maintenance windows for — a real increase in monthly patch volume, not just marketing about "AI-powered security."

Pentagon suspends CMMC Phase 2 requirements, launching a 60-day review of the program

📅 Jul 13, 2026

The Department of War (formerly Defense) immediately suspended the November 2026 deadline requiring third-party cybersecurity assessments across all defense contracts touching sensitive but unclassified data, citing a shortfall of roughly 100 qualified assessors against 100,000+ businesses needing certification. Phase 1 self-assessment requirements remain in force while a new task force studies scaled-back rules.

Why it matters: Defense contractors and the assessors who serve them should treat this as a pause, not a cancellation — expect a revised, likely lighter-weight framework within 60 days rather than a return to the original Phase 2 timeline.
