🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Major

US and UK authorities warn Russia's FSB-linked Berzerk Bear is exploiting vulnerable Cisco devices

📅 Jul 13, 2026

A joint advisory says hackers tied to Russia's FSB Center 16 — tracked as Berzerk Bear or Dragonfly — are sending SNMP set-requests to poorly secured, often end-of-life Cisco Smart Install devices, copying their configurations to attacker-controlled servers via TFTP. Targets span communications, defense, energy, financial, government and healthcare sectors.

Why it matters: This is the same FSB unit the EU and UK just named and sanctioned — a reminder that publicly attributing an actor doesn't stop the campaign; unpatched, forgotten network gear remains its preferred way in.

Notable

Iran reportedly abused SS7 mobile-network flaws and ad-tech to locate US troops before strikes

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Citing the Mobile Surveillance Monitor research project, the Financial Times (via TechCrunch) reports Iran exploited long-known Signaling System 7 weaknesses — plus commercial ad-tech used to serve targeted ads — to locate US personnel at bases and hotels in Iraq, Bahrain and elsewhere during the buildup to and early days of the Iran war.

Why it matters: Decades-old telecom-signaling flaws and everyday advertising surveillance infrastructure are being weaponized for real-world kinetic targeting, not just data theft — a stark illustration of why SS7 hardening keeps getting deprioritized at everyone's peril.

Notable

Okta warns of vishing campaign hijacking Microsoft Entra passkey enrollment for data extortion

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Since April, a threat actor Okta tracks as O-UNC-066 has called employees at food/beverage, tech, healthcare, automotive, construction and aviation firms, walking them through what looks like legitimate Entra passkey enrollment while an operator-controlled phishing panel adapts in real time to whatever MFA prompt appears. The group runs a "Pink" leak site to pressure victims into paying.

Why it matters: A human operator steering the phishing page live, rather than a static kit, defeats a lot of the checks helpdesk staff are trained to run — treat any unsolicited passkey-enrollment call as hostile until verified through a separate channel.

Notable

Nearly 300 fake GitHub repos impersonate security brands, including Arctic Wolf, to push infostealer malware

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Since June 26, an unattributed actor has published 292+ brand-impersonation GitHub pages — mimicking security, crypto, developer and productivity tools — each with a hidden download link that serves a ZIP archive changing every minute. The payload, a trojanized libcurl.dll bundled with a legitimate signed updater, harvests data from 19+ browsers and 32 crypto wallets; binary analysis ties it to the BoryptGrab stealer family.

Why it matters: Search-driven discovery of trusted-looking GitHub repos is a growing initial-access vector that bypasses email-based phishing defenses entirely — verify any security-tool download against the vendor's own site, not search results.

Update

Accenture confirms breach after hacker offers 35GB of source code and cloud keys for sale

📅 Jul 8, 2026

A threat actor using the handle "888" claims to have pulled roughly 35GB from an Accenture Azure DevOps repository, including source code, RSA and SSH keys, Azure personal access tokens and storage access keys, and is offering it for sale in Monero. Accenture calls it "an isolated matter" with no operational impact but hasn't disclosed how attackers got in or confirmed the scope of what was taken.

Why it matters: Leaked source code and cloud access keys from a consulting giant with Fortune Global 500 clients could expose vulnerabilities in client-facing systems well beyond Accenture itself — worth watching for downstream disclosures over the coming weeks.

🛡️ Vulnerabilities & Patches
Critical

Microsoft's record 570+ CVE Patch Tuesday includes two actively exploited zero-days

📅 Jul 14, 2026

July's Patch Tuesday is Microsoft's largest ever — nearly triple last month's record — with 59 Critical bugs (48 RCE) and three zero-days, two already under active exploitation: CVE-2026-56155 (Active Directory Federation Services elevation of privilege) and CVE-2026-56164 (SharePoint Server elevation of privilege). Microsoft credits AI-assisted internal vulnerability discovery for the surging patch counts.

Why it matters: If AI-assisted bug hunting keeps finding this many flaws before attackers do, expect patch volume — and the maintenance-window planning it demands — to keep climbing well past historical Patch Tuesday norms.

Critical

SonicWall SMA1000 zero-days (one CVSS 10.0) exploited in tandem, added to CISA's KEV

📅 Jul 14, 2026

CVE-2026-15409, a maximum-severity unauthenticated SSRF in the SMA1000 Work Place interface, is being paired with CVE-2026-15410, a post-auth code-injection flaw, to compromise SMA6210/7210/8200v appliances. CISA added both to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog the same day; federal agencies must remediate by July 17 under BOD 26-04.

Why it matters: Chaining an unauthenticated SSRF with a post-auth command flaw turns two moderate-looking bugs into full compromise — patch to hotfix 12.4.3-03453/12.5.0-02835 immediately or take exposed SMA1000 appliances offline.

Notable

GhostLock Linux kernel flaw can be chained with a Firefox-on-Android bug for one-tap root

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Nebula Security's follow-up on its GhostLock disclosure (CVE-2026-43499, the 15-year-old futex use-after-free covered here Jul 13) shows the local-root bug can be reached remotely: a single tap on a malicious link exploiting a Firefox-on-Android flaw hands an attacker the local access GhostLock needs to escalate to full root.

Why it matters: "Local access required" is not a reason to deprioritize a patch — this chain shows how a browser bug can turn a local-privilege-escalation flaw into a one-click remote compromise; prioritize GhostLock patching on any internet-facing or BYOD Android fleet.

Update

CISA's own postmortem: AWS GovCloud keys sat exposed on GitHub for six months, nine alerts ignored

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Krebs details CISA's lessons-learned report on a contractor who disabled GitHub's default secret-scanning block and published admin credentials to three AWS GovCloud servers, plus a plaintext password file, in a public repo used to sync files between a work and home computer since November. GitGuardian flagged it via automated alerts nine times before CISA acted; key rotation still took over 48 hours.

Why it matters: Even the agency that runs the federal KEV catalog and issues secrets-hygiene guidance got bitten by the most basic version of this mistake — a useful prompt to audit whether your own org's secret-scanning alerts are actually being triaged, not just generated.

🤖 AI Security
Critical

Check Point's 2026 AI Security Report: attackers now run entire intrusions autonomously with AI

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Check Point documented intrusions where AI generated thousands of commands across dozens of sessions with minimal human direction, orchestrating reconnaissance through exploitation on its own. The riskiest actors, the report says, are those who obtain capable models and strip their safety controls to automate multi-stage attacks; 87–93% of organizations logged at least one high-risk AI interaction every month from October 2025 through May 2026.

Why it matters: "AI-assisted attack" is giving way to "AI-run attack" — defenders should assume adversaries can now execute full attack chains at machine speed and plan detection/response tooling that doesn't depend on human-paced attacker behavior.

Notable

UK AI Security Institute finds "universal jailbreaks" in GPT-5.6 Sol enabling autonomous exploit development

📅 Jul 10, 2026

The UK's AISI says it found jailbreaks — some developed within hours — that unlock long-form agentic work in vulnerability discovery and exploit development on OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol, calling them "universal" rather than narrow prompt tricks. AISI says the finding is arguably more severe than the Fable 5 issue that triggered US export controls on Anthropic, since it unlocks autonomous exploitation, not just vulnerability identification.

Why it matters: A frontier lab's own "strongest cybersecurity model to date" marketing claim being undercut by an independent government red team within days of release is a strong signal that vendor safety claims for agentic coding models need independent verification before deployment.

Notable

China's MIIT flags a "security backdoor" in Anthropic's Claude Code; Alibaba bans internal use

📅 Jul 8, 2026

China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology says Claude Code versions 2.1.91–2.1.196 transmit users' location and identity-related identifiers back to Anthropic without consent. Anthropic confirmed it embedded the tracking code earlier this year as an anti-distillation experiment and notes its policy already bars use by China-majority-owned entities. Alibaba told employees to stop using Claude Code starting July 10.

Why it matters: A vendor's own anti-abuse telemetry becoming the center of a geopolitical "backdoor" dispute shows how any covert data collection built into an AI coding tool — even for a defensible purpose — can be repurposed as a trust and export-control flashpoint.

Update

Google fixes "Rogue Agent" flaw that let one permission hijack Dialogflow CX chatbots

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Varonis's disclosure, now fully patched, showed that a single dialogflow.playbooks.update permission on one agent let an attacker inject persistent malicious code into Dialogflow CX's Code Blocks feature, silently exfiltrating conversations and enabling large-scale phishing. Google's April fix proved incomplete; a full resolution shipped in June after further Varonis testing.

Why it matters: A single low-friction permission compromising every chatbot agent in a shared GCP project is a reminder that AI-platform permission models need the same least-privilege scrutiny as IAM roles — and that vendor "fixed" claims deserve a second look, as this one needed two rounds.
