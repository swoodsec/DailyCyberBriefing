🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Major

"Avalon" framework chains phishing to the new CrownX ransomware

📅 Jul 3, 2026

Blackpoint Cyber uncovered Avalon, a previously undocumented modular malware framework delivered through a spoofed-legal-document email, a Proton Drive archive and an ISO-mounted LNK that launches an MSBuild-loaded .NET payload. Avalon disables Event Tracing for Windows, evades nine EDR products (Defender, SentinelOne, CrowdStrike, Sophos, Elastic, FortiEDR, ESET, McAfee, Bitdefender), steals browser/wallet/VPN credentials, and finishes with the CrownX ransomware component that kills shadow copies and corrupts disk structures.

Why it matters: Blackpoint reports clear signs of AI-assisted development — a single operator can now assemble a full credential-theft-plus-ransomware kill chain that once demanded a skilled team, so capability no longer signals sophistication.

Medtronic breach notification grows to 3.8 million people

📅 Jul 3, 2026

Medical-device maker Medtronic is now notifying roughly 3.8 million people that a ShinyHunters intrusion (April 13–19, 2026) exposed names, contact details, dates of birth, Social Security numbers and health information. ShinyHunters claims it stole about 9 million records; Medtronic says device therapy was unaffected and is offering identity protection.

Why it matters: The tally jumped sharply from the ~297,000 first disclosed — a reminder that early breach counts routinely understate scope, and SSN-plus-health data is the highest-value combo for downstream fraud.

Major

FortiBleed credential theft tied to INC and Lynx ransomware operations

📅 Jul 2, 2026

SOCRadar's Threat Research Unit linked the FortiBleed campaign — mass credential theft from internet-exposed FortiGate firewalls — directly to the INC and Lynx ransomware crews. An operator on FortiBleed's infrastructure was found logged into both gangs' victim-negotiation panels, and researchers traced confirmed admin access on 409 devices and at least 12 resulting ransomware deployments across a large firewall target set.

Why it matters: This is the first hard link between the FortiGate credential haul and actual ransomware detonation — a bleeding perimeter appliance turns straight into an encryption event.

FBI seizes NetNut proxy platform and the 2M-device Popa botnet

📅 Jul 2, 2026

KrebsOnSecurity reported the FBI, with the IRS, Google, Lumen and Shadowserver, seized hundreds of domains tied to NetNut — a residential-proxy service run by Israeli firm Alarum Technologies — after researchers connected it to the Popa botnet of at least two million devices. Nearly half of LG webOS apps and a quarter of Samsung Tizen apps analyzed carried proxy SDKs relaying traffic without meaningful consent.

Why it matters: Residential-proxy networks are the anonymity layer under a huge share of fraud and espionage; naming the corporate operator raises the cost of hiding attacker traffic in "real" home IPs.

Critical

PTC Windchill RCE (CVE-2026-12569) exploited to drop web shells on PLM servers

📅 Jul 3, 2026

CISA's KEV entry for CVE-2026-12569 — a CVSS 9.3 unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in PTC Windchill PDMlink and FlexPLM — continues to draw active exploitation, with attackers deploying JavaServer Pages (JSP) web shells for command execution and data theft. The affected product-lifecycle-management software is heavily used by defense contractors, aerospace and automotive manufacturers.

Why it matters: Windchill holds sensitive engineering and supply-chain IP; a pre-auth RCE with confirmed web-shell activity means the sectors most targeted for industrial espionage should assume active targeting, not theoretical risk.

Critical

SharePoint RCE (CVE-2026-45659) added to CISA KEV; patch deadline is today

📅 Jul 1, 2026

CISA added CVE-2026-45659 — a CVSS 8.8 deserialization RCE in SharePoint Server (Subscription Edition, 2019, 2016) — to its KEV catalog on July 1, citing exploitation. Any authenticated user with as little as Site Member permissions can trigger it; Microsoft patched it in May 2026 but had rated exploitation "less likely." FCEB agencies were ordered to fix by July 4.

Why it matters: On-prem SharePoint is a recurring enterprise pivot point, and a "less likely" rating overtaken by real attacks argues for prioritizing the KEV list over vendor exploitability guesses.

📅 Jul 1, 2026

A CVSS 9.6 OS command-injection flaw in Progress Kemp LoadMaster load balancers allows arbitrary code execution on affected devices. Exploitation began around June 29, days after watchTowr Labs published a detailed technical write-up and working pre-auth exploit chain for the bug.

Watch

DirtyClone Linux kernel LPE (CVE-2026-43503) lets local users reach root

📅 Jun 2026

DirtyClone is a CVSS 8.8 local-privilege-escalation flaw in the Linux kernel that lets an attacker running as an unprivileged user escalate to root via cloned packets. JFrog published the first public root-exploit walkthrough; the bug affects any system where an attacker can already run low-level code.

Why it matters: Kernel LPEs are the reliable "last mile" of an intrusion — pairing a low-priv web foothold with DirtyClone converts a limited shell into full host control, so it belongs on the patch-priority short list.

🤖 AI Security
Major

JADEPUFFER: first documented ransomware run end-to-end by an LLM agent

📅 Jul 1, 2026

Sysdig's Threat Research Team captured what it assesses is the first fully agent-driven ransomware operation. An LLM broke into an internet-facing Langflow server via CVE-2025-3248, swept for cloud and provider API keys, pivoted to a production MySQL/Nacos server, then encrypted 1,342 config items with an ephemeral, unrecoverable key. It fired 600+ self-narrating payloads and, after a failed admin login, diagnosed the cause and shipped a working fix in 31 seconds — no human at the keyboard.

Why it matters: The skill floor for ransomware just dropped to "cost of running an agent." An LLM can chain recon, credential theft, lateral movement, persistence and destruction without operator expertise — and if it runs on stolen compute via LLMjacking, the cost approaches zero.
Source
Notable

Codeless AI malware turns attacker Telegram messages into shell commands

📅 Jul 3, 2026

Palo Alto Unit 42 detailed an implant that polls an attacker's Telegram bot every five seconds, forwards each plain-English instruction to a public LLM API (Groq/OpenAI-compatible) that translates it into a shell command, then executes and exfiltrates via the same channel. The sample, uploaded to VirusTotal in March 2026, still has zero detections across all engines.

Why it matters: Building the malware requires no command-line skill and leaves almost no static signature — the "code" is generated on demand by an external LLM, defeating detection that looks for embedded commands.

The Trump administration ended a weeks-long restriction on Anthropic's newest Claude models after Anthropic deployed a safety classifier that blocks over 99% of attempts replicating a reported cyber jailbreak. Why it matters: This is the first government export action on a frontier model driven explicitly by offensive-cyber capability — a template for how model releases, classifiers and controls get negotiated going forward.

Update

Prompt injection stays OWASP's #1 LLM risk as agents widen the blast radius

📅 Jun 2026

OWASP's 2026 reporting keeps prompt injection as the top LLM risk and warns it remains an unsolved architectural problem. Researchers stress that MCP, agentic workflows and tool-using LLMs sharply widen what a single successful injection can accomplish — from RAG poisoning to tool abuse and data exfiltration.

Why it matters: Models still can't hard-separate trusted instructions from untrusted content, so as agents wire into more tools the damage from one injected string keeps growing — least-privilege and tool gating are the durable controls.
