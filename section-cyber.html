🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Major

FortiBleed credential theft tied to INC and Lynx ransomware operations

📅 Jul 2, 2026

SOCRadar's Threat Research Unit linked the FortiBleed campaign — mass credential theft from internet-exposed FortiGate firewalls — directly to the INC and Lynx ransomware crews. An operator on FortiBleed's infrastructure was found logged into both gangs' victim-negotiation panels, and researchers traced confirmed admin access on 409 devices, a completed attack chain on 354, and at least 12 resulting ransomware deployments across a 430,000-firewall target set.

Why it matters: This is the first hard link between the FortiGate credential haul and actual ransomware detonation — a bleeding perimeter appliance turns straight into an encryption event.
Source
Notable

FBI seizes NetNut proxy platform and the 2M-device Popa botnet

📅 Jul 2, 2026

KrebsOnSecurity reported the FBI, with the IRS, Google, Lumen and Shadowserver, seized hundreds of domains tied to NetNut — a residential-proxy service run by Israeli firm Alarum Technologies — after researchers connected it to the Popa botnet of at least two million devices. Nearly half of LG webOS apps and a quarter of Samsung Tizen apps analyzed carried proxy SDKs that relayed traffic without meaningful consent; Google saw 316 threat-actor clusters using NetNut exit nodes in a single June week.

Why it matters: Residential-proxy networks are the anonymity layer under a huge share of fraud and espionage; taking one down and naming its corporate operator raises the cost of hiding attacker traffic in "real" home IPs.

Notable

Medtronic notifies ~297,000 patients after April intrusion claimed by ShinyHunters

📅 Jul 2, 2026

Medical-device maker Medtronic began notifying at least 297,307 people that an unauthorized actor accessed corporate IT systems between April 13–19, exposing names, contact details, dates of birth, Social Security numbers and health information. The ShinyHunters group claimed the breach and alleged 9 million records stolen; Medtronic says device therapy was unaffected and is offering two years of identity protection.

Why it matters: Health data plus SSNs is the highest-value combo for downstream fraud, and ShinyHunters' extortion pattern keeps landing major manufacturers regardless of device-safety assurances.

Notable

8.3 TB, 24-billion-record credential trove found exposed online

📅 Jun 2026

Cybernews researchers discovered a publicly exposed database holding more than 8.3 TB and roughly 24 billion credential records, aggregated from about 36 sources — Telegram channels, prior breach compilations, infostealer logs, and datasets apparently exported straight from live servers. It is one of the largest single credential aggregations documented to date.

Why it matters: Aggregated infostealer logs are the raw fuel for the password-spray and account-takeover campaigns hitting cloud identity right now; each mega-corpus resets the baseline for credential-stuffing risk.

🛡️ Vulnerabilities & Patches
Critical

SharePoint RCE (CVE-2026-45659) added to CISA KEV after active exploitation

📅 Jul 1, 2026

CISA added CVE-2026-45659 — a CVSS 8.8 deserialization remote-code-execution flaw in SharePoint Server (Subscription Edition, 2019, 2016) — to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on July 1, citing evidence of exploitation. Any authenticated user with as little as Site Member permissions can trigger it; Microsoft patched it in May 2026 but had rated exploitation "less likely." FCEB agencies were ordered to fix by July 4.

Why it matters: On-prem SharePoint is a recurring enterprise pivot point, and Microsoft's "less likely" rating being overtaken by real attacks is a reminder to prioritize the KEV list over vendor exploitability guesses.
Source
Critical

Progress Kemp LoadMaster command injection (CVE-2026-8037) exploited in the wild

📅 Jul 1, 2026

A CVSS 9.6 OS command-injection flaw in Progress Kemp LoadMaster load balancers allows arbitrary code execution on affected devices. Exploitation began around June 29, days after watchTowr Labs published a detailed technical write-up and working pre-auth exploit chain for the bug.

Why it matters: LoadMaster sits at the network edge in front of critical apps; a pre-auth RCE with public PoC gives attackers a fast, reliable foothold and a vantage point over the traffic it fronts.
Source
High

Splunk Enterprise pre-auth flaw (CVE-2026-20253) actively exploited

📅 Jun 18, 2026

CISA added CVE-2026-20253, a CVSS 9.8 missing-authentication flaw in Splunk Enterprise, to KEV and ordered rapid patching after confirming in-the-wild attacks. An unauthenticated PostgreSQL sidecar endpoint lets remote actors create or truncate arbitrary files and, under conditions, chain to RCE; watchTowr published a PoC on June 12, days after the fix.

Why it matters: Splunk is the SIEM at the center of many SOCs — compromising the logging platform lets an attacker both own the network and blind the defenders watching it.
Source
Watch

"RoguePlanet" Microsoft Defender zero-day (CVE-2026-50656) still awaiting a fix

📅 Jun 2026

Morphisec detailed RoguePlanet, a privilege-escalation flaw in the Microsoft Malware Protection Engine that can hand an attacker SYSTEM on a fully-updated Windows machine. Microsoft has confirmed the issue and says a patch is in development, but as of late June no fix had shipped — the third Defender EoP issue flagged in recent weeks.

Why it matters: When the bundled security engine itself is the escalation path, "keep AV updated" isn't enough; the detector becomes the attack surface and defenders need compensating controls until Microsoft ships.

🤖 AI Security
Notable

US lifts cyber ban on Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5

📅 Jul 1, 2026

The Trump administration ended a weeks-long restriction on Anthropic's newest Claude models after Anthropic deployed a new safety classifier that blocks over 99% of attempts replicating a reported cyber jailbreak. Fable 5 is again globally available; the more powerful Mythos 5 is restored only to a select group of federally-approved US organizations. Anthropic also published a draft AI jailbreak-severity framework and opened a HackerOne program for cyber-jailbreak reports.

Why it matters: This is the first time a government export action on a frontier model was driven explicitly by offensive-cyber capability — a template for how model releases, classifiers and controls will be negotiated going forward.
Source
Update

Prompt injection stays OWASP's #1 LLM risk, up 340% year over year

📅 Jun 2026

OWASP's 2026 reporting keeps prompt injection as the top LLM risk, citing a 340% year-over-year rise and its presence in an estimated 73% of production AI deployments. Researchers stress that MCP, agentic workflows and tool-using LLMs sharply widen what a single successful injection can accomplish — from RAG poisoning to tool abuse and data exfiltration.

Why it matters: Models still can't hard-separate trusted instructions from untrusted content, so as agents wire into more tools the blast radius of one injected string keeps growing — least-privilege and tool gating are the durable controls.

AI-enabled adversaries surge as machine speed compresses the patch window

📅 Jun 2026

CrowdStrike's 2026 threat reporting cites an 89% increase in attacks by AI-enabled adversaries and a fastest-recorded eCrime breakout time of 27 seconds, while Google's threat group tracks adversaries using AI for zero-day discovery and autonomous malware. Why it matters: When both discovery and exploitation accelerate, the assumption that defenders have days-to-weeks after disclosure breaks down — pushing the industry toward automated remediation and faster patch pipelines.
