Mira Murati's Thinking Machines debuts Inkling, a 975B open-weight model

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Thinking Machines Lab — the startup founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati — released Inkling, an Apache-licensed Mixture-of-Experts model with 975 billion total parameters (about 41B active), trained on 45 trillion tokens of text, image, audio, and video. The company concedes it's "not the strongest overall model available," betting instead that enterprises want AI they can download, fine-tune, and customize; full weights are on Hugging Face and it's live for tuning on its Tinker platform.

Why it matters: A high-profile new lab planting its flag in open weights — rather than chasing the closed frontier — reinforces the week's central theme: the value is shifting from the single smartest model to cheap, customizable intelligence developers control.

Major

Moonshot's Kimi K3 tops open benchmarks as weights are promised July 27

📅 Jul 17, 2026

Moonshot AI's 2.8-trillion-parameter open MoE, Kimi K3, ranked first on the Frontend Code Arena — ahead of Claude Fable 5 — and posted 88.3% on Terminal-Bench 2.1, trailing only GPT-5.6 Sol, at roughly 40% lower cost than US frontier tiers. Moonshot says it will release the full open weights by July 27, letting companies and governments run and customize the model themselves.

Why it matters: A Chinese open model taking the No. 1 spot on a coding leaderboard, at a fraction of the price and with downloadable weights, is the concrete event that turned "China is catching up" into "China may be ahead" across Washington and Silicon Valley this week.

Rebuilt Gemini 3.5 Pro misses a third deadline as Google eyes a stopgap

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.5 Pro slipped past its widely-reported July 17 general-availability target — its third consecutive miss after June and an earlier July date — and remains in limited preview for a handful of enterprise customers, with no model card or public API listing. Reporting says the model fell short of Google's internal reliability and hallucination bars after a ground-up rebuild, with DeepMind now weighing a stopgap release.

Why it matters: As rivals ship on schedule and Chinese open models seize the leaderboard, Google's repeated slips on its flagship raise the stakes — and every spec and date still comes from leaks, not an official announcement.

OpenAI's GPT-Red red-teamer beats humans 84% to 13% on prompt injection

📅 Jul 15, 2026

OpenAI detailed GPT-Red, an automated red-teaming model trained via self-play at the compute scale of its largest post-training runs, which found successful attacks in 84% of scenarios where human red-teamers managed 13%. It surfaced a previously unseen "fake chain of thought" attack — injecting a spoofed reasoning trace into another model — and was used to adversarially harden GPT-5.6; OpenAI is keeping it internal because of its offensive capabilities.

Why it matters: Saturated safety evals are a real bottleneck, and a model that out-hacks human teams to fix its own vulnerabilities is exactly the scalable oversight labs need as agents gain autonomy — while also concentrating powerful attack know-how inside the labs.

AI race splits in two as China wages an "open-weight insurgency"

📅 Jul 18, 2026

On OpenRouter — a major marketplace routing developers across competing systems — Chinese models now occupy the top five spots by weekly token usage, all of them open-weight releases from Tencent, Xiaomi, DeepSeek, MiniMax and Z.ai. Axios's thesis: most enterprise work (routine coding, summarization, extraction, support) doesn't need the smartest model, and one investor estimates open-source could soon handle 95% of enterprise queries.

Why it matters: If cheap, customizable open models absorb the bulk of real-world demand, building the single best frontier model stops being a winning strategy — reframing US labs' prestige systems as expensive tools for a shrinking sliver of the hardest problems.

An independent hands-on read of Kimi K3, pelican benchmark and all

📅 Jul 16, 2026

AI engineer Simon Willison put Kimi K3 through his own informal evaluations — including his running "SVG of a pelican riding a bicycle" test — offering an early, vendor-independent look at how the largest open model actually behaves versus its benchmark headlines. His writeup weighs the model's coding and reasoning strengths against the practical realities of running a 2.8T-parameter open release.

Why it matters: As labs increasingly cite their own preferred benchmarks, credible independent hands-on evals are what keep "state of the art" claims honest — and Willison's quirky, reproducible tests have become a widely-watched reality check on new releases.

29 countries sign a China-led World AI Cooperation Organization

📅 Jul 17, 2026

On the eve of Shanghai's World AI Conference, 29 countries — including Russia, Pakistan, Indonesia, Kazakhstan, Brazil and Serbia — signed the founding agreement for the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO), an intergovernmental body to be headquartered in Shanghai, with UN Secretary-General António Guterres present. In his keynote, Xi Jinping called AI development "a symphony of international cooperation" and pledged 5,000 AI training slots for developing countries over five years.

Why it matters: As US labs coalesce around a Washington-led standards body, Beijing is standing up a rival, Global-South-facing governance bloc the same week — turning the fight over AI rules into an openly geopolitical contest over who writes them.

David Sacks warns the US risks losing the AI race after Kimi K3

📅 Jul 17, 2026

White House AI adviser and investor David Sacks wrote that for the first time a Chinese model — Kimi K3 — has taken the top spot on the Frontend Code Arena and is "scoring at or near the frontier" elsewhere, arguing the US is "tying itself in knots" with data-center bans and pre-approval rules for model releases. He insists America still wins if it sticks with a "pro-innovation, pro-infrastructure, pro-energy and pro-export" agenda.

Why it matters: When the administration's own AI point person publicly frames a Chinese open model as an inflection point, it signals policy pressure building toward looser domestic constraints and a harder export push — with direct consequences for how and where US labs can ship.
