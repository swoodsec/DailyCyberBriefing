💼 Business & Policy
Major

China's AI companion law takes effect, forcing Doubao and Qwen to disable humanlike agents

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Beijing's Interim Measures for the Administration of Artificial Intelligence Anthropomorphic Interaction Services took effect today, requiring bot disclosure, emotional-distress interventions, an under-14 ban, and easy exit options. ByteDance's Doubao (345M monthly users) and Alibaba's Qwen shut down custom-persona and humanlike agent features; Doubao gives users read-only data access until October 15, while Qwen's agent configurations and chat histories are being permanently deleted with no migration path.

Why it matters: China just became the first major market to legally force AI products to stop pretending to be human — a regulatory line Western labs, unconstrained by any equivalent rule, haven't had to cross.

26 current and former Meta employees sue over AI-driven layoff targeting

📅 Jul 14, 2026

A federal lawsuit filed in Oakland alleges Meta used AI tools — including its "Metamate" assistant, an internal "second brain" tracking employee communications, and a productivity score built from keystrokes, screen content, and AI-token usage — to select workers for 2026 layoffs in ways that disproportionately hit employees with disabilities or medical leave. Meta says workforce decisions "were and are made by people, not AI."

Why it matters: This is one of the first major lawsuits to allege AI-mediated discrimination inside a Big Tech layoff process itself — a preview of the legal exposure every company now using AI productivity scoring in HR decisions is carrying.
