🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Major

FBI and Google dismantle NetNut, a 2-million-device residential proxy botnet

📅 Jul 7, 2026

The FBI, working with the IRS Criminal Investigation division, seized hundreds of domains tied to NetNut, a residential proxy service run by publicly-traded Israeli firm Alarum Technologies. Google's Threat Intelligence Group and Lumen tied NetNut to "Popa," an Android-based botnet of at least 2 million hijacked smart TVs and streaming boxes running since 2022; in one week alone GTIG observed 316 distinct threat-actor clusters using NetNut exit nodes for account takeovers, password spraying and espionage-linked access-masking. The takedown cut off NetNut's Google-based command-and-control and pushed Play Protect updates to block the proxy SDK.

Why it matters: Residential proxy networks are the connective tissue that lets both criminal and nation-state actors blend in with legitimate home traffic — dismantling one at this scale removes a shared piece of infrastructure many different threat actors depended on.

🛡️ Vulnerabilities & Patches
Critical

"Bad Epoll" Linux kernel flaw gives any local user root, reaches Android and Chrome's sandbox

📅 Jul 3, 2026

CVE-2026-46242, dubbed "Bad Epoll," is a use-after-free race condition in the Linux kernel's epoll subsystem, present since a 2023 code change and affecting kernels 5.10 through 6.11. Researcher Jaeyoung Chung built a working exploit with a reported ~99% reliability rate; because epoll cannot be disabled, there is no workaround, and the bug is reachable even from inside Chrome's renderer sandbox, which blocks most other kernel exploits. A fix is available and should be applied immediately across Linux servers, desktops and Android devices.

Why it matters: A near-guaranteed root exploit with no mitigating workaround, reachable from browser sandboxes, is about as bad as local privilege escalation gets — this belongs at the top of every Linux and Android patch queue this week.
Source
High

Unpatched Argo CD repo-server flaw can still hand attackers a full Kubernetes cluster

📅 Jul 6, 2026

Synacktiv's unauthenticated RCE in Argo CD's repo-server component remains unfixed and un-CVE'd after being reported to maintainers in January 2025. The repo-server's internal gRPC service has no authentication; a crafted GenerateManifest request substitutes a malicious script for the expected Helm/Kustomize call, and Helm-chart installs leave Kubernetes network policies off by default — so a single compromised pod can reach the repo-server and read the cluster's Redis password.

Why it matters: With no patch available, GitOps control planes need immediate network-policy isolation around the repo-server and Redis ports — this is now a live, public exploit path with no vendor fix on the horizon.
Source
High

New CitrixBleed-style NetScaler flaw exploited within 24 hours of disclosure

📅 Jul 1, 2026

CVE-2026-8451 (CVSS 8.8) is an out-of-bounds read in NetScaler's XML parser affecting appliances configured as a SAML IDP: an unquoted XML attribute followed by a newline causes the parser to read past its buffer and leak memory — including session cookies — into the NSC_TASS response cookie. Scottish firm Lupovis observed exploitation begin less than 24 hours after Citrix's June 30 patch, with attackers filtering targets by HTTP response code to hit only genuinely vulnerable, SAML-enabled hosts.

Why it matters: NetScaler's SAML-parsing bugs keep echoing the CitrixBleed pattern that fueled mass ransomware intrusions in 2023 — patch immediately or disable SAML IDP if patching isn't possible yet.
Source

📅 Jul 1-7, 2026
