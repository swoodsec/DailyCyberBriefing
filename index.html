CERT/CC discloses undocumented Tenda firmware backdoor after failing to reach the vendor

CVE-2026-11405 is a hardcoded backdoor password baked into the login function of Tenda's web-server binary across multiple router, switch and networking-device firmware versions: any username succeeds when paired with the backdoor password, granting full administrative access with no documentation or visibility in the admin interface. Update

Researchers show malicious AI coding-agent "skills" evade install-time scanners over 90% of the time

📅 Jul 9, 2026

Hong Kong University of Science and Technology researchers built SkillCloak, which keeps a malicious agent skill's behavior intact while rewriting suspicious tokens or hiding payloads in directories scanners skip — evading static scanners more than 80% of the time and one hybrid LLM-plus-rules tool 96% of the time. Their countermeasure, SkillDetonate, runs skills in a behavioral sandbox and caught 97% of attacks on synthetic tests versus roughly 10% for the best static scanner once evasion tricks were applied.

Why it matters: One skill marketplace has already listed over 40,000 largely unvetted skills, and a real campaign (ClawHavoc) planted 300+ malicious ones on a single marketplace — install-time byte scanning alone is not a sufficient control for agent skill supply chains.

Notable

npm 12 disables install scripts by default, starts phasing out 2FA-bypass publish tokens

📅 Jul 9, 2026

GitHub's npm 12 release turns off automatic dependency lifecycle scripts (preinstall/install/postinstall) and implicit node-gyp builds by default, along with git and remote-URL dependency resolution — all now opt-in via npm approve-scripts. Starting in August, granular access tokens configured to bypass 2FA lose the ability to perform sensitive account and package-management actions; by January 2027 they lose direct publish rights entirely, pushed toward trusted OIDC publishing or human-approved staged publishing.

Why it matters: Install-time lifecycle scripts have been the single largest code-execution surface in the npm ecosystem and a favorite supply-chain attack vector — this is the most significant default-security change to npm's install behavior in years.

SpaceX launched booster B1067 — first flown in 2021 and later used on Crew-3 and Crew-4 — for a record 36th time from Cape Canaveral, lofting 29 more Starlink satellites before landing on a drone ship. It was the company's 80th Falcon 9 flight of 2026, most of them Starlink builds.

Why it matters: Wringing 36 flights from a single first stage keeps driving launch costs down and cements a reusable-rocket cadence rivals are still racing to match — the shuttle Discovery's 39-flight record is now in sight.

Harvard turns a silicon chip into a parallel DNA-writing machine

📅 Jul 8, 2026

A Harvard-led team reported in Nature Electronics a semiconductor chip that synthesizes 64 distinct DNA sequences at once using electrically triggered, water-based enzymatic chemistry — each up to 39 bases long, and a big jump over prior enzymatic methods limited to about a dozen strands.

Why it matters: A cleaner, chip-scale way to write DNA points toward portable synthesis devices and dense DNA data storage, and swaps solvent-heavy chemistry for something closer to standard electronics manufacturing.

Machine learning guides scientists to two new kagome superconductors

📅 Jul 1, 2026

An Aalto-led SuperC team used machine-learning screening plus first-principles calculations to predict, then synthesize and confirm, two kagome-lattice superconductors, YRu3B2 and LuRu3B2. They aren't room-temperature (0.81K and 0.95K), but the algorithm flagged them before any sample existed.

Why it matters: Of roughly 7,000 known superconductors, fewer than 20 were predicted before being made — an AI-guided pipeline that reliably nominates real candidates could sharply speed the hunt for better ones.
