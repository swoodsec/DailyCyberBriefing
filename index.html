🚨 Breaches & Active Threats
Major

FortiBleed credential theft tied to INC and Lynx ransomware operations

📅 Jul 2, 2026

SOCRadar's Threat Research Unit linked the FortiBleed campaign — mass credential theft from internet-exposed FortiGate firewalls — directly to the INC and Lynx ransomware crews. An operator on FortiBleed's infrastructure was found logged into both gangs' victim-negotiation panels, and researchers traced confirmed admin access on 409 devices, a completed attack chain on 354, and at least 12 resulting ransomware deployments across a 430,000-firewall target set.

Why it matters: This is the first hard link between the FortiGate credential haul and actual ransomware detonation — a bleeding perimeter appliance turns straight into an encryption event.

Notable

FBI seizes NetNut proxy platform and the 2M-device Popa botnet

📅 Jul 2, 2026

KrebsOnSecurity reported the FBI, with the IRS, Google, Lumen and Shadowserver, seized hundreds of domains tied to NetNut — a residential-proxy service run by Israeli firm Alarum Technologies — after researchers connected it to the Popa botnet of at least two million devices. Nearly half of LG webOS apps and a quarter of Samsung Tizen apps analyzed carried proxy SDKs that relayed traffic without meaningful consent; Google saw 316 threat-actor clusters using NetNut exit nodes in a single June week.

Why it matters: Residential-proxy networks are the anonymity layer under a huge share of fraud and espionage; taking one down and naming its corporate operator raises the cost of hiding attacker traffic in "real" home IPs.

Why it matters: Health data plus SSNs is the highest-value combo for downstream fraud, and ShinyHunters' extortion pattern keeps landing major manufacturers regardless of device-safety assurances.

Critical

SharePoint RCE (CVE-2026-45659) added to CISA KEV after active exploitation

📅 Jul 1, 2026

CISA added CVE-2026-45659 — a CVSS 8.8 deserialization remote-code-execution flaw in SharePoint Server (Subscription Edition, 2019, 2016) — to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on July 1, citing evidence of exploitation. Any authenticated user with as little as Site Member permissions can trigger it; Microsoft patched it in May 2026 but had rated exploitation "less likely." FCEB agencies were ordered to fix by July 4.

Why it matters: On-prem SharePoint is a recurring enterprise pivot point, and Microsoft's "less likely" rating being overtaken by real attacks is a reminder to prioritize the KEV list over vendor exploitability guesses.

A CVSS 9.6 OS command-injection flaw in Progress Kemp LoadMaster load balancers allows arbitrary code execution on affected devices. Exploitation began around June 29, days after watchTowr Labs published a detailed technical write-up and working pre-auth exploit chain for the bug.

Splunk Enterprise pre-auth flaw (CVE-2026-20253) actively exploited

📅 Jun 18, 2026

CISA added CVE-2026-20253, a CVSS 9.8 missing-authentication flaw in Splunk Enterprise, to KEV and ordered rapid patching after confirming in-the-wild attacks. Why it matters: Splunk is the SIEM at the center of many SOCs — compromising the logging platform lets an attacker both own the network and blind the defenders watching it.
