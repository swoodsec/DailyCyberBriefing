IBM shares crash 25% — worst day ever — after Q2 earnings warning

📅 Jul 14, 2026

IBM pre-announced preliminary Q2 revenue of $17.2 billion, well short of the $17.85 billion analysts expected, and adjusted EPS of $2.93 versus a $3.01 estimate. The stock sank about 25% to roughly $217 — its steepest one-day fall on record — as weak mainframe (Z) demand and delayed large contracts bit; full results land July 22.

Why it matters: IBM blamed clients diverting late-June capex toward supply-constrained servers, storage and memory ahead of price hikes — a vivid sign of how the AI-infrastructure scramble is warping enterprise IT budgets.

New York becomes first U.S. state to pause new hyperscale data centers

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Governor Kathy Hochul signed an executive order temporarily halting state environmental permits — for up to a year — for new "hyperscaler" data centers drawing 50 megawatts or more, while the state writes a regulatory framework to protect ratepayers, the grid and the environment. Four such centers already operate in New York, with 39 more applications pending.

Why it matters: It is the nation's first statewide data-center moratorium, and a template other states could copy just as AI build-outs collide with rising utility bills and strained power grids.
Washington Post

EU clears €659M German state aid for four "first-of-a-kind" chip plants

📅 Jul 14, 2026

The European Commission approved €659 million in German subsidies under the Chips Act for four semiconductor facilities: €353M for silicon carbide in Baesweiler, €214M for power MOSFETs in Itzehoe, €74M for metrology tooling in Weilburg and €18M for two specialized Munich fabs, backing Element 3-5, Vishay, KLA and KETEK.

Why it matters: Europe keeps writing checks to rebuild domestic chip capacity and reduce reliance on Asian and U.S. suppliers, targeting the power and metrology niches that feed AI and EV hardware.

Apple in talks with startup PrismML to shrink big AI models onto the iPhone

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Apple is in early discussions with Caltech spinout PrismML, whose compression claims to squeeze Alibaba's 27-billion-parameter Qwen model from ~54GB to under 4GB so it can run entirely on an iPhone 15 or newer. The company says its method cuts memory 10–15x and boosts speed 6–8x while losing only a few points of accuracy.

Why it matters: Running server-class models on-device — without a cloud round-trip — is central to Apple's privacy-first AI pitch, and could reset what "on-device intelligence" means for phones.

July Pixel update finally kills the Android 17 boot-loop bug

📅 Jul 8, 2026

Google's first monthly patch since Android 17 shipped fixes the notorious boot loop that had trapped Pixels in constant restarts since the March Feature Drop, plus crashes on app launch, wrong-colored system widgets and foldable navigation-bar glitches. The staggered rollout reaches 21 models, from the Pixel 6 to the Pixel 10a.

Why it matters: The boot loop had bricked usable phones for months; the fix is a reminder of how fragile major OS jumps can be — and Google is also offering a Pixel Repair Tool for devices still stuck.

📅 Jul 15, 2026

China's Interim Measures on "anthropomorphic interaction" AI services take effect today, requiring anti-addiction systems, usage warnings and instant-exit mechanisms. 📅 Jul 14, 2026

Microsoft's July 14 Patch Tuesday build ships a raft of features: a Point-in-Time Restore that rolls a PC back to a known-good state via Volume Shadow Copy, the option to pause Windows Update indefinitely, a full-screen "screen tint" accessibility overlay, quieter Widgets, IPP-default printer setup and faster File Explorer launches.

Microsoft's on-premises SharePoint Server 2016 and 2019 — and the Project Server builds on them — hit end of extended support July 14, with no more security patches, cumulative updates or technical support. Astronauts take the first diagnostic X-rays in space

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Results published in Radiology confirm that Fram2 crew members captured the first-ever medical X-rays in orbit using a portable device, imaging a hand, chest, abdomen, pelvis and even a smartwatch. Though slightly lower quality than ground scans, the images were good enough to diagnose injuries like broken bones — taken by non-medical astronauts after just four hours of training.

Why it matters: Portable, spaceflight-proven X-ray imaging is a prerequisite for keeping crews healthy on long Moon and Mars missions far from any hospital.
Space.com

NASA awards nearly $600M for four commercial Moon landings in 2028

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Under its Commercial Lunar Payload Services program, NASA is paying Astrobotic ($297.9M for two deliveries), Intuitive Machines ($148.3M) and Firefly Aerospace ($144.2M) to land the same trio of instruments — studying landing-plume dust, radiation and lunar navigation — near a planned Moon Base by late 2028.

Why it matters: NASA is doubling down on buying rides from private landers rather than building its own, laying scientific groundwork for a permanent human presence on the Moon.

SpaceX flies a reused Falcon booster for the 600th time

📅 Jul 13, 2026

SpaceX launched Starlink batches from opposite coasts about eight hours apart on July 13–14, with the second flight marking the 600th use of a flight-proven booster. The milestone came on booster B1080's 28th mission from California; both flights deployed their satellites successfully.

Why it matters: Six hundred reuses turns rocket reusability from a stunt into routine industrial cadence — the economics underpinning the entire Starlink and commercial-launch build-out.

Hubble and Webb find the first of 10,000 "missing" black holes in Omega Centauri

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Using 20-plus years of Hubble data plus new Webb observations, astronomers detected oMEGACat BH-2, the first stellar-mass black hole confirmed in the giant Omega Centauri cluster. At 4.46 solar masses and locked in the longest-period black-hole binary known, it hints at a hidden population models say should number around 10,000.

Why it matters: Finding these long-predicted but elusive black holes tests how star clusters — and the black holes within them — form and evolve over cosmic time.
Space.com
