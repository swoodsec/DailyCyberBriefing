Uber strikes a $14.8B deal to buy Delivery Hero

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Uber agreed to acquire Frankfurt-listed Delivery Hero for €41.50 a share in cash, an equity value of about $14.8 billion, in a combination spanning 99 markets with roughly $236 billion in combined 2025 gross bookings. Delivery Hero's boards unanimously back the offer, and Uber will divest the company's businesses in 14 markets to SSW Partners for about $1.6 billion.

Why it matters: The deal nearly doubles Uber's global delivery reach and would create the largest food-delivery platform outside China — a big consolidation bet just as sector growth cools, and it faces a long regulatory review before an expected 2027 close.

TSMC posts record profit, lifts capex, and adds $100B to its Arizona build-out

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Taiwan Semiconductor reported record Q2 net income of NT$706.6 billion, up 77.4% year over year, on revenue of $40.2 billion, and raised its 2026 capital-spending outlook to $60–64 billion. CEO C.C. Why it matters: As the sole foundry for the most advanced AI chips, TSMC's record results and back-to-back capex hikes are the clearest signal yet that the AI hardware buildout is still accelerating — and its Arizona expansion deepens the U.S. onshoring push.

Zoox recalls 105 robotaxis after one drove into heavy smoke at a fire scene

📅 Jul 17, 2026

Amazon-owned Zoox issued a software recall covering all 105 of its robotaxis after an unoccupied vehicle drove into heavy smoke obscuring an active, un-coned emergency fire scene in Las Vegas on June 20. The over-the-air fix follows an NHTSA directive ordering AV developers to reliably yield to first responders.

Why it matters: It's a fresh reminder that scaling driverless fleets hinges on edge-case perception — smoke, flares, flashing lights — and regulators are now pressing the whole industry on how AVs behave around emergencies.

Quantum computing tackles a key fusion-fuel material for the first time

📅 Jul 2026

Researchers from Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Cleveland Clinic, and IBM ran the first known quantum-computer calculations on FLiBe — a molten salt of fluorine, lithium, and beryllium — successfully modeling nine molecular configurations relevant to producing the tritium future fusion plants will need.

Why it matters: It's an early but concrete example of quantum computers doing useful chemistry, and it targets one of fusion energy's hardest supply problems: making enough tritium to sustain a reactor.

Why it matters: A practical room-temperature superconductor would transform power grids and computing; ML-guided materials discovery is quietly compressing what used to be years of trial-and-error into a far faster screening loop.

Physicists extend "magnon" lifetimes ~100x, boosting a quantum-computing path

📅 Jun 26, 2026

Researchers stretched the lifespan of magnons — tiny magnetic waves once dismissed as too short-lived — from a few hundred nanoseconds to as long as 18 microseconds, nearly 100x longer, by improving material quality, turning them into plausible carriers of quantum information.

Why it matters: Longer-lived magnons open an alternative route to compact quantum devices, adding to the growing menu of physical platforms competing to make practical quantum computing real.
