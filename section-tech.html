U.S. official says "very few" Nvidia H200 chips have reached China

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Under Secretary of Commerce Jeffery Kessler told a congressional hearing Tuesday that only "a very small quantity" of Nvidia's H200 AI accelerators have shipped to China and Hong Kong since Washington began granting export licenses, with roughly 10 approved buyers including Alibaba, Tencent and ByteDance.

Intel pours €5B into its Irish fab, Europe's sole volume EUV plant

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Intel announced a €5 billion ($5.7B) capital investment to expand output at its Leixlip, Ireland campus, upgrading existing cleanrooms and installing leading-edge tools to build Xeon 6 and next-gen Xeon chips on its Intel 3 node. The sum is nearly 30% of Intel's ~$17B 2026 capital budget; Fab 34 remains the only EUV fab running at volume anywhere in Europe.

Why it matters: Rather than build a new plant from scratch, Intel is densifying capacity it already owns to chase AI and high-performance-computing demand — a bet that reinforces Europe's fragile semiconductor supply chain.

Apple shares have climbed about 15% since June 25, adding nearly $600 billion in market value and returning to record territory. 📅 Jul 9, 2026

Meta will begin manufacturing Iris, the fourth generation of its custom MTIA silicon, in September on TSMC's 3nm process, according to an internal memo. Why it matters: Every custom accelerator Meta ships is a step away from Nvidia and AMD dependence, and a marker of how aggressively hyperscalers are verticalizing their AI hardware.

EU takes a step toward restricting social media for under-13s

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Commission President Ursula von der Leyen said she's convinced by the case for "age-appropriate" limits, unveiling an expert report that found European children spend four to six hours a day on social platforms. The proposed tiered approach would let under-13s use apps only for limited periods under adult supervision; formal proposals go to member states after the summer.

Why it matters: A bloc-wide age gate across 27 countries would force platforms to overhaul age verification and reopen the fight over how far governments should police kids' access to the feed.

Prefect buys Dagster, uniting the two big Airflow successors

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Prefect agreed to acquire Dagster Labs, combining the two most widely adopted modern replacements for Apache Airflow into one orchestration suite serving thousands of data teams. The company frames the stack around three layers — Dagster defines outcomes, Prefect executes, FastMCP governs access — with Dagster keeping its name, open-source license and roadmap.

Why it matters: Consolidating the leading data-and-AI workflow orchestrators reshapes the tooling that production ML and agent pipelines depend on, and thins the field of independent Airflow challengers.

Linux 7.2-rc3 lands, tracking Torvalds' "new normal"

📅 Jul 12, 2026

Linus Torvalds released the third 7.2 release candidate, calling activity "normal" for the summer slowdown. Roughly half the changes are driver work, alongside Ultra RISC-V support in the default RISC-V config, multi-GPU display-detection fixes, SEGA Dreamcast driver patches and a growing share of AI-assisted fixes. The stable 7.2 kernel is on track for August.

Why it matters: The kernel underpins most of the world's servers, cloud and Android devices, and each cycle's quiet plumbing — new architectures, hardware enablement, AI-assisted patches — sets what runs everywhere downstream.

SpaceX targets July 16 for Starship Flight 13 after FAA clears it

📅 Jul 14, 2026

With the FAA having closed its investigation into the previous Starship flight, SpaceX is aiming for a July 16 liftoff of Flight 13 from Starbase, Texas — the second launch of the Version 3 vehicle powered by 33 Raptor 3 engines. The mission will deploy 20 next-gen Starlink V3 satellites, six of them fitted with cameras to inspect Starship's heat shield in flight.

Why it matters: Version 3 is the configuration SpaceX intends to fly faster, reuse harder and eventually send toward the Moon and Mars, so each successful test shortens the runway to routine heavy-lift operations.

NASA moves its SunRISE solar mission to a Falcon Heavy

📅 Jul 14, 2026

NASA said its SunRISE heliophysics mission will now ride a SpaceX Falcon Heavy from Kennedy Space Center rather than ULA's Vulcan Centaur. The six toaster-oven-size SmallSats will fly in formation just above geosynchronous orbit, acting as one giant radio telescope to track solar radio bursts from energetic particle events that threaten satellites and astronauts.

Why it matters: The swap is another sign of Falcon Heavy's growing pull for government science payloads, and the mission itself sharpens space-weather forecasting as launch cadence and human spaceflight both ramp.

A 200-year-old light trick spins up "optical skyrmions" for future computing

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Nanyang Technological University researchers created exotic swirling light structures called optical skyrmions using the Poisson spot — the bright dot at the center of a disc's shadow, known since the 1800s — instead of costly engineered metamaterials. Shining a laser at a simple circular disc generated up to four kinds of topological field patterns at once, per the study in Optica.

Why it matters: Skyrmions are a hot candidate for packing information densely, so a cheap, simple way to make optical ones could feed future data-storage, communications and computing systems.
ScienceDaily
