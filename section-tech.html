Uber strikes a $14.8B deal to buy Delivery Hero

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Uber agreed to acquire Frankfurt-listed Delivery Hero for €41.50 a share in cash, an equity value of about $14.8 billion, in a combination that spans 99 markets with roughly $236 billion in combined 2025 gross bookings. Delivery Hero's boards unanimously back the offer, and Uber will separately divest the company's businesses in 14 markets to SSW Partners for about $1.6 billion.

Why it matters: The deal nearly doubles Uber's global delivery reach and creates the largest food-delivery platform outside China — a major bet on consolidation just as the sector's growth cools, though it faces a long regulatory review before an expected 2027 close.

TSMC posts record profit, lifts capex, and adds $100B to its Arizona build-out

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Taiwan Semiconductor reported a record Q2 net income of NT$706.6 billion, up 77.4% year over year, on revenue of $40.2 billion, and raised its 2026 capital-spending outlook to $60–64 billion from $52–56 billion. CEO C.C. Wei also announced an additional $100 billion investment in Arizona — lifting TSMC's total U.S. commitment there to $265 billion — and lifted full-year revenue growth guidance to above 40%.

Why it matters: As the sole foundry for the most advanced AI chips, TSMC's record results and back-to-back capex hikes are the clearest read yet that the AI hardware buildout is still accelerating — and its expanded Arizona footprint deepens the U.S. push to onshore leading-edge chipmaking.

Netflix beats on earnings but a soft outlook drags shares to a one-year low

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Netflix reported Q2 revenue of $12.56 billion, up 13.4%, and net income of $3.4 billion (80 cents a share), edging past profit estimates on membership growth, pricing, and ad revenue it still expects to double to $3 billion this year. But guidance for 11.7% Q3 growth undershot Wall Street, and the stock fell as much as 9% after hours to its lowest level in more than a year.

Why it matters: After two years of strong gains, investors are now scrutinizing whether Netflix's advertising and pricing engine can keep offsetting slowing engagement growth — and a cautious outlook was enough to break the momentum.

Google will let Android users install rival app stores from Google Play on July 22

📅 Jul 15, 2026

After Google and Epic Games jointly withdrew their latest bid to alter the court order in Epic's antitrust win, Google confirmed that starting July 22, U.S. users can download competing app stores directly from within Google Play instead of through sideloading. Developers' U.S. listings will automatically become available to rival stores unless they opt out, and third-party store operators pay a $5,000 annual fee.

Why it matters: It's the most consequential opening of Android's app economy yet — pitting Google's long-standing 30% commission against stores like Epic's that charge as little as 12%, and setting a template regulators elsewhere will watch closely.

A last-second engine anomaly aborts SpaceX's Starship Flight 13

📅 Jul 16, 2026

SpaceX reached Super Heavy ignition at Starbase but telemetry showed four booster engines failed to start as planned, triggering an automatic abort seconds before liftoff. The flight was set to deploy the first 20 production Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory; Elon Musk said a next attempt could come "in a few days."

Why it matters: Flight 13 is meant to prove Starship's V3 vehicle can deploy operational satellites, a milestone toward the orbital flights NASA is counting on for Artemis 3 — so even a clean pad abort resets a tight cadence SpaceX hopes to run monthly.

Webb spots Beta Pictoris d, the faintest exoplanet ever directly imaged

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Using the NIRSpec instrument on the James Webb Space Telescope, astronomers identified a third giant planet — Beta Pictoris d, at least twice Jupiter's mass — orbiting the intensely studied young star some 30 astronomical units out. It's the first directly imaged planet found primarily through moderate-resolution spectroscopy rather than traditional coronagraphic imaging.

Why it matters: Detecting a planet by its atmospheric "fingerprint" in a crowded, dusty system proves a new technique for finding worlds that conventional imaging misses, expanding how and where astronomers can hunt for exoplanets.

Quantum computers model fusion-fuel material for the first time

📅 Jul 6, 2026

Researchers at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Cleveland Clinic, and IBM calculated nine molecular configurations of FLiBe — a molten salt central to breeding tritium in future fusion reactors — the first known instance of such computations run on quantum computers. The work, tied to the U.S. Genesis Mission, blends high-performance and quantum computing to speed materials discovery.

Why it matters: Simulating fusion-relevant chemistry on quantum hardware is an early but concrete example of quantum computing tackling a problem beyond classical reach, and points toward faster design of the materials commercial fusion will require.
