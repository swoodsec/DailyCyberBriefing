Google loses EU court fight over €4.1B Android antitrust fine

📅 Jul 2, 2026

The European Union's top court dismissed Google's appeal and upheld a €4.1 billion ($4.7 billion) penalty, agreeing that the company abused Android's market power — the record fine the European Commission first imposed in 2018 for tying its search and browser apps to the mobile operating system.

Why it matters: The final ruling closes one of the EU's landmark tech-antitrust cases and reinforces regulators' authority over how dominant platforms bundle their software, setting the tone as DMA enforcement expands.

📅 Jul 2, 2026

Tesla reported its strongest second quarter ever, delivering 480,126 vehicles against a Wall Street consensus near 406,000 — beating even the most bullish forecasts by more than 60,000 units and ending two straight years of delivery declines. Why it matters: After two years of shrinking sales, a sharp rebound driven by stronger European demand resets the narrative around Tesla's core auto business — even as BYD stays ahead in global EV volume.

Supreme Court overrules Humphrey's Executor, giving the president power to fire FTC commissioners at will

📅 Jun 29, 2026

In a 6-3 decision in Trump v. Slaughter, the Court struck down the FTC's 91-year-old "for-cause" removal protections, holding that commissioners serve at the president's pleasure and effectively ending the requirement that the agency be bipartisan. The ruling reverses the 1935 Humphrey's Executor precedent while preserving the Federal Reserve's independence.

Why it matters: The FTC is the lead US antitrust and privacy enforcer against Big Tech; making its members removable at will hands the White House direct sway over how aggressively tech mergers and platform conduct get policed.

Microsoft begins rolling out the redesigned Windows 11 Start menu with July's Patch Tuesday

📅 Jul 2, 2026

Microsoft is enabling its overhauled Start menu for Windows 11 versions 24H2 and 25H2, starting with the July 2026 cumulative update. The redesign drops the split pinned/all-apps layout for a single vertically scrollable panel with category-based app browsing and finer control over Recommended content — the biggest change to the launcher since the OS shipped, delivered mostly via configuration rather than a large download.

Why it matters: The Start menu is the most-used surface in Windows, and reworking it for a billion-plus devices is a rare, high-visibility bet — one Microsoft is pairing with a pullback of Copilot prompts from Notepad, Photos, and other built-in apps.

Notable

NASA readies a rescue mission to grapple and re-boost its aging Swift space telescope

📅 Jul 2, 2026

NASA's Swift Boost mission will send the LINK satellite — built by Arizona's Katalyst Space Technologies — to rendezvous with, capture, and tow the Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory into a stable orbit before its decaying trajectory drags it into the atmosphere. An air-launch attempt aboard Northrop Grumman's Stargazer aircraft was scrubbed by a launch-vehicle issue.

Why it matters: On-orbit servicing to save a working science satellite is a live demonstration of a new commercial market — refueling, repairing, and repositioning spacecraft instead of letting them deorbit.
