Microsoft launches biggest-ever Xbox reset: ~3,200 jobs cut, five studios divested

📅 Jul 6, 2026

Microsoft is cutting roughly 3,200 gaming roles — about 20% of the unit — and divesting studios including Compulsion, Double Fine, Ninja Theory and Undead Labs, with Arkane exploring options. New Xbox CEO Asha Sharma cited bloated management, weak Game Pass growth and thin margins.

Why it matters: The retreat reverses years of aggressive studio acquisitions and shows even Big Tech gaming must streamline as AI infrastructure spending reshapes capital allocation.

Meta faces up to $1.4 trillion penalty bid in states' youth-addiction case

📅 Jul 6, 2026

In a Monday court filing, Meta asked a federal judge to strike penalty calculations from California, Colorado, Kentucky and New Jersey seeking as much as $1.4 trillion — a figure near Meta's market cap — over claims Facebook and Instagram were designed to addict minors. Meta called the number "untethered" from any wrongdoing.

Why it matters: The staggering figure underscores intensifying legal and regulatory pressure on social platforms to redesign features affecting young users.

Chipmakers hit with US price-fixing suit as memory "RAMpocalypse" bites

📅 Jun 30, 2026

A federal class action accuses Samsung, SK Hynix and Micron of colluding to restrict consumer DRAM supply while steering capacity to AI's high-bandwidth memory, allegedly pushing prices up as much as 700% over four years. Jefferies expects DRAM to climb another 40-50% this quarter.

Why it matters: Rising memory costs ripple into the price of nearly every PC, phone and server, and the AI buildout is now warping the entire components supply chain.

High

Machine learning discovers two new superconductors, opening a faster search

📅 Jul 7, 2026

The SuperC consortium, led by Aalto University's Päivi Törmä, used ML to screen vast material combinations and, with Rice University, synthesized and verified two previously unknown superconductors (YRu3B2 and LuRu3B2). Their critical temperatures are near absolute zero, but the method could scale searches into the billions of candidates.

Why it matters: A workable room-temperature superconductor would slash energy losses across the grid and data centers; ML-guided discovery may be the shortcut that finally gets there.
Phys.org
