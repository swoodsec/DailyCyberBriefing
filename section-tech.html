Global startup funding hits a record $510B in H1 2026 — but two AI labs took 43%

📅 Jul 2, 2026

Crunchbase data shows venture investors poured $510 billion into startups in the first half of 2026, surpassing the $440B invested in all of 2025 and topping the prior half-year peak (H2 2021). OpenAI and Anthropic alone accounted for $217 billion — 43% of the total — while more than 70% of Q2 capital went to AI-focused companies.

Why it matters: The number confirms a historic capital cycle, but its extreme concentration in a handful of frontier labs is the real story — it strains the rest of the ecosystem even as IPOs and M&A finally return in force.

Onsemi strikes ~$7B all-stock deal for Synaptics in a "physical AI" push

📅 Jun 25, 2026

ON Semiconductor agreed to buy Synaptics for nearly $7 billion in an all-stock deal — its largest acquisition ever — to add on-device (edge) AI compute and wireless connectivity to its power-chip and sensor portfolio. The company says the deal expands its addressable market by $30B to $243B by 2030; it is expected to close in mid-2027.

Why it matters: "Physical AI" — putting intelligence into robots, cars, and factory hardware — is becoming the semiconductor industry's next battleground, and consolidation is how legacy chipmakers assemble the full stack to compete.

Rocket Lab agrees to buy satellite-comms pioneer Iridium for $8 billion

📅 Jun 29, 2026

Launch company Rocket Lab inked a landmark $8 billion deal ($54/share) to acquire Iridium, the Virginia-based operator of an L-band LEO constellation serving 2.5 million voice, IoT, aviation, maritime, and defense customers. CEO Peter Beck called it "the start of a new era," folding a recurring-revenue satellite network into Rocket Lab's vertically integrated launch and manufacturing business.

Why it matters: Rocket Lab is transforming from a small-rocket launcher into an end-to-end space company and defense contractor, and Iridium's spectrum and direct-to-device ambitions put it into the fast-growing satellite-connectivity race.

Meta plans to rent out spare AI compute — and neocloud stocks tumble

📅 Jul 1, 2026

Meta is reportedly building a cloud business, "Meta Compute," to sell access to its hosted models and raw GPU capacity — putting it in direct competition with AWS and neocloud providers like CoreWeave. Shares of CoreWeave and Nebius fell about 12% each on the report, while Meta rose more than 10%.

Why it matters: Meta guided to $115B–$135B in 2026 capex; turning idle build-out into revenue would let it recoup a massive AI outlay — and signals the hyperscaler that once rented from neoclouds may soon undercut them.

Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron sued in US over alleged DRAM price-fixing amid "RAMpocalypse"

📅 Jun 25, 2026

A class action filed in the Northern District of California (Garciaguirre v. Samsung) accuses the three DRAM makers of coordinating supply and pricing since 2022, driving memory costs up roughly 700% over four years. Plaintiffs allege the trio curtailed DDR3/DDR4 output under cover of a coordinated shift to high-bandwidth memory (HBM) for AI.

Why it matters: Record memory prices are already inflating the cost of every PC, phone, and server; a successful suit could reshape a market where three "oligopolists" — two of them prior price-fixing offenders — control most of the world's DRAM.

Samsung and SK Hynix anchor South Korea's ~$518B semiconductor mega-project

📅 Jun 29, 2026

Samsung and SK Hynix will each build two new fabs in South Korea's southwest as part of an 800 trillion won (~$518B) national semiconductor-ecosystem plan, with the two companies contributing roughly $260B apiece. It sits within a broader Samsung/SK commitment reported at up to $1.3 trillion over the next decade.

Why it matters: The scale of state-backed chip investment underscores how central memory and AI silicon have become to national industrial strategy — and how much manufacturing capacity is being locked in for the AI era.

Final Atlas V lofts 29 Amazon Leo satellites, clearing the way for broadband service this year

📅 Jul 2, 2026

A United Launch Alliance Atlas V lifted off from Cape Canaveral at 12:30 a.m. EDT July 2 carrying 29 Amazon Leo (formerly Project Kuiper) satellites — the last Atlas V flight for the constellation before ULA transitions to its Vulcan rocket. About 400 satellites are now in orbit across 15 missions, and Amazon says it now has enough to begin commercial service.

Why it matters: Amazon's megaconstellation is finally reaching operational scale, setting up the first serious challenger to SpaceX's ~11,000-satellite Starlink in the fast-growing LEO broadband market.

NASA readies a first-of-its-kind rescue to grapple and re-boost its aging Swift telescope

📅 Jul 2, 2026

NASA is paying about $30 million for the Swift Boost mission, in which Arizona's Katalyst Space Technologies will fly a servicing satellite to rendezvous with, capture, and tow the Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory into a stable orbit before its decaying trajectory drags it into the atmosphere.

Why it matters: On-orbit servicing to save a working science satellite is a live demonstration of an emerging commercial market — refueling, repairing, and repositioning spacecraft instead of letting them deorbit.

Physicists design quantum controls that can reshape the "arrow of time"

📅 Jun 25, 2026

Researchers (including at Los Alamos) published protocols in Physical Review X that generate quantum processes more consistent with time flowing backward than forward. By precisely managing continuous measurements and Hamiltonian controls, the system can even recapture the energy injected by measurement and store it — like a battery.

Why it matters: Beyond the eye-catching "time reversal" framing, the work points toward new ways to prepare and stabilize quantum states and to harvest energy from measurement — both useful primitives for building more practical quantum computers.
