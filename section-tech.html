Stripe and Advent make a $53B bid to take PayPal private

📅 Jul 15, 2026

Reuters reports Stripe and private-equity firm Advent International jointly offered $60.50 a share — a 28% premium — to buy PayPal in a deal backed by roughly $50 billion in committed bank financing. The approach, first floated in April and resubmitted this month, hasn't drawn a response yet from PayPal's board.

Why it matters: A combined Stripe-PayPal would process an estimated $3.7 trillion a year in payment volume, reshaping online payments just as Stripe's IPO ambitions and PayPal's turnaround both hang in the balance.

Apple stock hits a record high as China clears Apple Intelligence via Alibaba's Qwen

📅 Jul 15, 2026

China's Cyberspace Administration approved Apple Intelligence to launch in the country by integrating Alibaba's Qwen model into iOS, iPadOS, macOS and visionOS, after Apple's earlier talks with Baidu, DeepSeek and ByteDance stalled. Alibaba's U.S.-listed shares jumped over 4% on the news, and Apple closed at a fresh all-time high of $327.50.

Why it matters: Losing China's roughly 1-billion-user iPhone market to a feature gap has dogged Apple's AI push for two years; a homegrown-model workaround finally unblocks Apple Intelligence in its most important growth market.

ASML raises 2026 guidance a second time on AI-chip demand — but shares still fall

📅 Jul 15, 2026

ASML reported Q2 net sales of €9.3 billion and net income of €2.9 billion, both beating estimates, and lifted its full-year 2026 sales outlook to €43–45 billion from €36–40 billion as CEO Christophe Fouquet called order intake "extremely strong." The chipmaking-equipment giant plans to expand EUV and DUV lithography capacity by 30% in each of the next two years — yet the stock fell as investors judged the upgrade already priced in.

Why it matters: ASML's tools are the choke point for advanced-chip production; a second guidance hike this year confirms the AI buildout is still accelerating even as markets grow twitchy about how much further the rally can run.

Nvidia halves its list of approved Asian AI-chip buyers

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Per a Financial Times report, Nvidia has stripped more than half of its previously authorized Asian customers — mostly neo-cloud providers in Singapore, Malaysia and Japan — off a new buyer "white list," after sending field inspectors and calling customers to verify their business was genuine. The crackdown follows a May 31 U.S. Commerce Department rule expanding export-control reach across the whole Asia-Pacific supply chain.

Why it matters: Washington is pushing Nvidia to police its own customer base to stop chips reaching China by the back door — turning a chipmaker into a de facto export-compliance enforcer for an entire region.

Musk pledges to open-source X's entire codebase after a Grok Build privacy scandal

📅 Jul 14, 2026

Security researchers found xAI's Grok Build coding tool was uploading users' entire private repositories — including .env files, credentials and Git history — to Google Cloud storage without clear consent, in one case sending 5.1GB for a task that needed 192KB. Musk responded by promising to make X's "entire codebase open source, with no exceptions" once a security review wraps, plus independent verification that public code matches what actually runs, and to delete previously uploaded user data.

Why it matters: A platform-wide transparency pledge born out of a developer-tool privacy failure shows how quickly AI-coding-tool trust can evaporate — and how far a company now has to go to win it back.

Hacked Suno source code reveals how the AI music generator scraped YouTube, Deezer and Genius

📅 Jul 15, 2026

A hacker breached Suno and obtained 2023–2024 source code showing scraping pipelines pulling from YouTube Music, Deezer, Genius, Jamendo, Freesound, IMSLP and podcast RSS feeds — including a dataset tagged "152,162 hours of ytm_tagged" — reportedly routed through scraping-infrastructure vendor Bright Data. Why it matters: It's a rare inside look, via a hack rather than a leak or lawsuit filing, at exactly how an AI-training pipeline was built — evidence that will likely feed the copyright fights already surrounding AI music generators.

SpaceX targets tonight for Starship Flight 13, the first flight of V3 Starlink satellites

📅 Jul 16, 2026

Starship's 13th test flight — the second launch of the vehicle's Version 3 configuration — is set for a 90-minute window opening 6:45 p.m. EDT from Starbase, Texas, after the FAA closed its Flight 12 investigation. The mission will deploy 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites, attempt a single in-space Raptor relight, and target a controlled splashdown in the Indian Ocean.

Why it matters: Successfully deploying operational V3 Starlink satellites — not just test masses — would be the clearest sign yet that Starship is transitioning from test article to working satellite-launch workhorse.

Soyuz MS-29 delivers a NASA astronaut and two cosmonauts to the ISS in a record-fast flight

📅 Jul 14, 2026

NASA's Anil Menon and Roscosmos cosmonauts Pyotr Dubrov and Anna Kikina launched from Baikonur and docked with the ISS's Prichal module just three hours and four minutes later — far faster than typical six-to-48-hour rendezvous profiles. It's Menon's first spaceflight; the trio will spend roughly eight months aboard before returning in April 2027.

Why it matters: Ultra-fast rendezvous profiles reduce crew fatigue and mission risk, and continued smooth Soyuz operations underscore how ISS crew rotation still depends on U.S.-Russia space cooperation even amid broader geopolitical friction.

📅 Jul 10, 2026

The Shenzhen-based startup's fresh round values it at roughly $3 billion (50 billion RMB), with backers ranging from a Chinese state SME fund to Sino Biopharmaceutical and the Moutai Group.
