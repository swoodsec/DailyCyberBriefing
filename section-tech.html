Bezos' Blue Origin seeks $10B in first outside raise at a $130B valuation

📅 Jul 8, 2026

Blue Origin is raising $10 billion in its first-ever external funding round at a $130 billion pre-money valuation, with Coatue expected to lead at about $4 billion and Bezos himself adding roughly $2 billion. The raise follows the late-May test explosion of its New Glenn rocket and SpaceX's blockbuster IPO last month.

Why it matters: Opening a famously self-funded company to outside investors signals that even a Bezos-backed space venture needs deep external capital to compete — and puts a market price on the No. 2 player in commercial launch.

Micron commits up to $3B to reinforce the U.S. chip supply chain

📅 Jul 9, 2026

Micron announced plans to invest up to $3 billion in the U.S. semiconductor ecosystem, including $500 million in strategic financing for GlobalWafers' 300mm silicon-wafer plant in Sherman, Texas, plus a 10-year supply deal. Shares rose about 7% on the news.

Why it matters: Locking up domestic raw-wafer capacity is a hedge against the supply crunch and price spikes hammering the memory market, and a bet that AI-driven demand for advanced memory keeps climbing.

SpaceX flies its most-traveled Falcon 9 booster a record 36th time

📅 Jul 9, 2026

SpaceX launched booster B1067 — first flown in 2021 and later used on Crew-3 and Crew-4 — for a record 36th time from Cape Canaveral, lofting 29 more Starlink satellites before landing on a drone ship. It was the company's 80th Falcon 9 flight of 2026, most of them Starlink builds.

Why it matters: Wringing 36 flights from a single first stage keeps driving launch costs down and cements a reusable-rocket cadence rivals are still racing to match — the shuttle Discovery's 39-flight record is now in sight.

Harvard turns a silicon chip into a parallel DNA-writing machine

📅 Jul 8, 2026

A Harvard-led team reported in Nature Electronics a semiconductor chip that synthesizes 64 distinct DNA sequences at once using electrically triggered, water-based enzymatic chemistry — each up to 39 bases long, and a big jump over prior enzymatic methods limited to about a dozen strands.

Why it matters: A cleaner, chip-scale way to write DNA points toward portable synthesis devices and dense DNA data storage, and swaps solvent-heavy chemistry for something closer to standard electronics manufacturing.
ScienceDaily
Update

Machine learning guides scientists to two new kagome superconductors

📅 Jul 1, 2026

An Aalto-led SuperC team used machine-learning screening plus first-principles calculations to predict, then synthesize and confirm, two kagome-lattice superconductors, YRu3B2 and LuRu3B2. They aren't room-temperature (0.81K and 0.95K), but the algorithm flagged them before any sample existed.

Why it matters: Of roughly 7,000 known superconductors, fewer than 20 were predicted before being made — an AI-guided pipeline that reliably nominates real candidates could sharply speed the hunt for better ones.
ScienceDaily
