Chip stocks shed $1T-plus as Nvidia slips below $5 trillion on AI-spending doubts

📅 Jul 7, 2026

Semiconductor shares sold off sharply after Samsung's Q2 revenue fell short of Wall Street's high AI bar, with Micron down ~13%, Intel ~9% and AMD ~7%, briefly pushing Nvidia's market cap under $5 trillion. Analysts tie the reset to doubts about returns on record AI capital spending, dot-com-level valuations and a more hawkish Fed.

Why it matters: The selloff is the first serious market test of whether the AI infrastructure boom can keep justifying its valuations — and it ripples straight into the cost and supply of memory and processors.

Apple loses EU court fight; App Store and iOS remain DMA "gatekeepers"

📅 Jul 8, 2026

The EU's General Court in Luxembourg dismissed all three of Apple's challenges to its gatekeeper designations, upholding the Commission's decision to treat the App Store and iOS as core platform services under the Digital Markets Act and ruling its iMessage claim inadmissible. Apple is expected to appeal to the EU's top court.

Why it matters: The ruling cements the DMA's reach over Apple's ecosystem and signals that gatekeepers must comply first and litigate later, with fines of up to 10% of global revenue on the line.
9to5Mac

Google loses final EU appeal over €4.1B Android antitrust fine

📅 Jul 2, 2026

The EU's top court, the Court of Justice, upheld the €4.1 billion ($4.7B) penalty for abusing Android's market power, ending an appeal that ran since the fine was first levied in 2018. The decision is legally binding.

Why it matters: Back-to-back losses for Google and Apple show Europe's competition regime hardening into an enforceable check on the biggest platforms — a template regulators elsewhere are watching.

📅 Jul 9, 2026

SpaceX launched booster B1067 — first flown in 2021 on a NASA cargo run and later on Crew-3 and Crew-4 — for a record-setting 36th time from Cape Canaveral, carrying 29 more Starlink satellites. A successful drone-ship landing marked SpaceX's 635th booster recovery.

AI simulation cracks how neutron-star mergers forge the heaviest elements

📅 Jul 8, 2026

An international GSI/FAIR team trained a deep-learning model to simulate the r-process nuclear reactions that build heavy elements during neutron-star collisions — one of nuclear astrophysics' hardest computations — far faster than conventional methods. The work appeared in Physical Review D.

Why it matters: Faster, accurate merger models tighten the link between telescope observations and lab experiments, sharpening our picture of where gold, platinum and other heavy elements come from.
ScienceDaily

Webb traces interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS to an ancient, pre-solar origin

📅 Jun 22, 2026

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope measured unusually high deuterium and scant carbon-13 in interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, suggesting it formed in a cold, dense cloud perhaps 10–12 billion years ago — likely predating our solar system. The findings were published in Nature.

Why it matters: Only the third confirmed interstellar visitor, 3I/ATLAS offers a rare direct sample of chemistry from another star system and clues to how common life's ingredients may be.
NASA Science
