Apple sues OpenAI, alleging trade-secret theft "at every level"

📅 Jul 10, 2026

Windows 11's big July 14 update brings Point-in-Time Restore

📅 Jul 13, 2026

Microsoft's July 2026 update (KB5095093) rolls out July 14 with 13 notable additions, led by Point-in-Time Restore — an automatic recovery feature that snapshots settings, files and apps via Volume Shadow Copy to roll a PC back to a known-good state. It also lets users pause updates indefinitely and adds accessibility and File Explorer improvements.

Why it matters: A one-click system rollback and indefinite update pausing address two of Windows users' oldest frustrations — botched updates and forced restarts — signaling a more user-controllable direction for the OS.

A quantum computer models fusion fuel chemistry for the first time

📅 Jul 6, 2026

Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Cleveland Clinic and IBM computed nine molecular configurations of FLiBe — a molten fluorine-lithium-beryllium salt used to breed the tritium that fusion reactors need — using IBM's 156-qubit Heron processor paired with ORNL supercomputing. It's the first known quantum-computer calculation of fusion-fuel materials.

Why it matters: Simulating the electron behavior of complex materials is where classical machines choke, so a working quantum-plus-supercomputing pipeline hints at a practical near-term use for quantum hardware — and at speeding the hunt for viable fusion fuels.
The Quantum Insider

Scientists solve why lithium dendrites crack solid-state batteries

📅 Jul 10, 2026

Researchers confirmed that soft lithium dendrites fracture hard ceramic electrolytes through hydrostatic pressure — lithium forcing into pre-existing nanoscale cracks left by standard sintering — rather than via electron leakage along grain boundaries. The finding points to tougher electrolytes, engineered micro-voids and protective anode coatings.

Why it matters: Short-circuiting dendrites are the central obstacle to commercial solid-state batteries, so pinning down the mechanism gives engineers concrete paths toward the safer, longer-lasting cells long promised for EVs and phones.
ScienceDaily
Update

Euclid telescope spots some of the universe's most ancient quasars

📅 Jul 6, 2026

ESA's Euclid space telescope, with NASA contributions, imaged 31 of the oldest quasars ever documented — including two that are the oldest yet observed, dating to when the universe was just 5% of its current age, and a dozen from the first 770 million years after the Big Bang.

Why it matters: Catching supermassive black holes blazing so early deepens the puzzle of how they grew so fast, and showcases the wide-field survey power Euclid and the incoming Roman telescope will bring to mapping the early cosmos.
NASA Science
